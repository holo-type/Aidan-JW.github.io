Scanner Art (2022)

Poetry Untitled freewrite (2022) Gnawing at roots to deprive the above of what's beneath. It never stops, the cycles turning like grindstones Amidst a vast system turning and churning Waves and fire. But to find a speck That shines on a ray of golden nothing On a backdrop of green Nothing can be better, I think And not many can find. Can this special moment Be stripped, pried from the cold Rocky hands Of crumbling statues Which we appear to be And fool ourselves Into being? Is it so is it nothing, everything A blackness eternal And the lake of fire in the dandelions that sprout On the fresh mornings we delight to rise to On days which eat us away. End the cycle a common theme But the greatest fear Locked away Forever isolated on an island Of sculpted iron. Halls (2022) Dare to find those signs of life that lie across these sacred halls Laid to rest, and kept to waste Indifferent to your mortal calls Among them find a cry ring out Slapping silent cross sanctum walls I'm here, it shouts, with glassy gaze Reflecting through immortal thralls. Interloper! Your search is over Come to find At last forgotten A cry, a silent cry Absorbed and swallowed shut The everlasting voiceless Unchanging Untouched Untitled freewrite (2023) Simplicity commanded. Enacted, overshared still remnants remain Of something sitting cold still smoking in the ash How long will this take? To be consumed by what was wanted Is wanted Shall attain but still greater things Wrapped in shiny foil that glistens Like rain Like blood Like something else entirely. Matte painted, spread thin Robustness overlooked by many not more Of these things left suspended In the bottom of a glass Where does it all go? Asks the question that knows not it's a question Flashing and flashing But no extravagance or romance Can penetrate the dull depths Of what consumes the most precious thing A thing forever slipping Through cracks undetected Running deeper still Than the hardened creases of the palm.
